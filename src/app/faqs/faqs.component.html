How were the study areas chosen?

Twelve case study areas were selected as the locations for detailed landscape-scale simulation case studies, based on the project criteria of exploration of climatic, population and land use variations across southern Australia.

Where does the data come from?

The data presented here are based on the combined output of these main steps:

Phoenix Simulation
Impact Analysis
Cost Estimation
Bayesian Decision Network
Voila! The Answer

Fire behaviour simulation is based on the Phoenix RapidFire model, and incorporates different prescribed burning treatment rates and locations, different weather streams and different ignition locations, as well as base layers supplied by state and territory fire management agencies. Impact estimation is based on published techniques for relating fire behaviour and other outputs from Phoenix to values such as life and property loss, infrastructure damage and vegetation sensitive to particular fire intervals.

Risk estimation is based on Bayesian Decision Networks, which estimate the risk to each value at each treatment level from the simulation-derived distributions of data and provide a basis for comparison between regions that explicitly incorporates the observed frequency of fire weather conditions in each case study area. For more information, see Cirulis et al. 2019.
How accurate are the results?
As with any experiment that involves modelling, the results reflect the strengths and weaknesses of the model and datasets used to drive the model. For details of the models and datasets, including limitations, see Cirulis et al. 2019 and the annual project reports at the BNHCRC.
